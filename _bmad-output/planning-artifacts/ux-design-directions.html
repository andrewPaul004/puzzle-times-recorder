Direction Comparison

Direction Home hero Best for Trade-off
01 Warm Journal Chronological diary — each solve is a dated entry Emma: diary-keepers, habit-formers, MSP migrators who want to relive history Marcus has no obvious entry point; marketplace buried
02 Activity Dashboard Streak + stats header, quick log CTA, recent solves Emma: performance-oriented users; feels familiar from fitness apps Header can feel like a SaaS dashboard; warm register is harder to maintain
03 Cozy Collection Visual puzzle grid — the collection as a shelf Marcus and lapsed puzzlers: browse-first, visual, collectable feel Emma's time-focused log is not the hero; streak and PBs are buried
04 Minimal Timer The timer itself — distraction-free, task-focused The log interaction as a showcase; best as a drill-down screen, not a home Not a home screen direction — works best as the timer state within another direction
05 Social Feed Friend activity — community and belonging first Users who come for community; drives return visits via social pull Cold start problem: empty feed on day one is the worst first impression
06 Passport Identity The Puzzle Passport — earned identity and badges Identity-forward users; high shareability; strong for Priya's advocacy path Less action-oriented; Emma's "log now" impulse competes with a profile view

Note on combining directions: Direction 04 (Minimal Timer) is a screen state, not a home screen — it belongs inside any of the other directions as the active logging experience. Directions 01 and 02 are the strongest candidates for Emma's primary IA. Elements from Direction 06 (Passport) work well as the Profile tab within any home direction.
